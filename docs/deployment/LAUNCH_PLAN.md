## TL;DR — What we're doing in one paragraph

We're launching Bassicology publicly as a **funnel-aligned product**, not just a tutorial SKU library. The YouTube channel opens this week with one original groove per week. The video link points to a **whitelist email-capture page** during the pre-launch phase (~6 weeks), while we build the funnel-pure platform: a free playable tier with **capped levers** (every control works once — the AHA), Membership ($24/mo) that uncaps everything, a Funk Vol. 1 Pack ($39), and an Economy Picking Accelerator ($97) with hands-on 1:1 cohort guidance. The platform IS the instrument — both on YouTube and on the page — and the whole funnel obeys one rule: **don't watch, practice.**

## The one belief everything hangs on

Bass education has spent fifteen years confusing **watching** with **learning**. Everyone makes videos _about_ grooves. Lectures. Demos.

Bassicology is built the other way: **almost nothing to watch, everything to do.** That belief isn't just our product philosophy — it's our funnel philosophy. The funnel itself has to obey the rule. The free experience puts the bass in their hands _before_ any pitch ever shows up.

> **They describe practice. We _are_ practice.**

## The funnel shape — brain speed slows as trust deepens

```
Shorts (IG + YT, 90 mph)   →  win 8 bars, make them grab the bass
        ↓
Weekly YouTube (50 mph)    →  one groove, deconstructed live, played WITH them
        ↓
Three doors → the platform →  the AHA, then assessment, then account
        ↓
Email sequence (20 mph)    →  a daily rep in the inbox, then the offer escalates
        ↓
The platform               →  playable free; depth, tools, programs paid
```

### The three doors

Different traffic temperatures need different doors. Each door catches a source the others can't.

| Door                            | Traffic                     | Mechanism                       | AHA happens...    |
| ------------------------------- | --------------------------- | ------------------------------- | ----------------- |
| **1 — Landing Page**            | COLD (ads, search)          | Fast static page → assessment   | In the assessment |
| **2 — Weekly YouTube**          | WARM (10-min platform demo) | Click → exact groove cued up    | During the video  |
| **3 — Pain-Point SEO/GEO Blog** | HIGH-INTENT search          | Playable widget _is_ the answer | On the blog page  |

**Critical rules:**

- Doors point _forward into the platform_, never sideways into each other.
- Don't promote slow content on fast platforms.
- The SEO door chases _doing_ queries ("why does MY bass sound muddy"), not informational queries — AI Overviews kill those.
- Optimize for AI engine citations (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini), not just Google rank.

### Door 2 in detail — the heart of the content engine

We don't make videos _about_ the platform. **The platform IS the instrument we play on camera.** On screen, live: set up the groove, slow it down, speed it up, pitch it up, switch to drummer-only, then click-only, mute the bass and take the seat.

A 10-minute video breathes between **two modes:**

- **Reveal + explanation** — watching, fine, teaches the device, creates the desire to practice.
- **Play-along** — "grab your bass, four counts in, we're playing this together." _This part is practice, on camera._

**The built-in wall:** Video can only run at one tempo, one key, one pass. It can't loop the bar they keep flubbing, mute our bass, or score them. Those limits _are_ the pitch.

**The handoff:** "Keep practicing with these controls — link below" isn't a gear-shift into a pitch; it's "go use the thing you just watched work." Click → account → the exact groove is waiting, already cued up.

> **The one discipline:** _play_ the interface, never _operate_ it. Every control you touch must be because the groove needs it right now — never a UI tutorial.

## The free/paid constitution

> **The platform is fully _playable_ on a free account. Access is free. Depth, tools, and programs are paid.**

The line is **taste vs unlimited**, never **practice vs no practice**. Putting practice behind the paywall would hide the AHA — the exact moment that converts a cold visitor — so the funnel would stop converting. The free user practices for real; they just keep hitting the ceiling, and paying removes it.

### The permanent gating rule

> **If it's part of _playing a groove_ → free (capped). If it's _depth on_, _a tool beside_, or _a program around_ playing → paid.**

- Playing a groove (play, mute, take the seat, one pull of each lever) → **free**
- Unlimited depth (full tempo range, all keys, infinite loop, scoring) → **paid**
- Tools beside playing (ear training widgets, sight reading, notation) → **paid**
- Programs around playing (technique packs, accelerator, future programs) → **paid**

### The free-groove wall — taste, never the whole pie

The wall doesn't live around the catalog; it lives **inside each lever.**

> **A lever they can pull once is a taste. A lever they can live on is the pie.**

| Lever                     | Free (the taste)               | Paid (the uncap)              |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------ | ----------------------------- |
| **Mute & jam**            | Mute the bass once, jam 8 bars | Loop infinitely, every groove |
| **Tempo**                 | One slower preset and back     | Full 40–200 BPM dial          |
| **Transpose**             | Shift to one other key         | All 12 keys                   |
| **Deconstruction layers** | See them exist (greyed)        | Isolating & drilling them     |
| **Scoring / Bridge**      | _(deferred to v24)_            | _(deferred to v24)_           |

**Each cap teaches the specific upsell.** When they hit the tempo wall, they learn exactly what they're buying. The cap must feel like a **generous teacher** ("here's how much more of this real thing exists"), never a **fake locked button** ("gotcha").

The test: would a player _remark_ on it to another musician? "It actually transposed, and I realized the paid version does all 12 keys" sells itself. "It showed me a fake locked button" breaks trust.

### Let them collect — don't fear it

A user who clicks every free link across many videos and ends up with 60 free grooves is the **dream, not the danger.** Sixty _capped_ grooves is sixty _demos_ — sixty run-ins with the wall. The bigger their free library, the stronger the membership pitch.

## The "earn the next groove" gate — interim, until Bridge ships

The funnel vision calls for gating the next groove on **Bridge scoring** ("you can play this now"). Bridge is deferred to **v24**, so we need an interim mechanism.

**The interim gate: "played in full N times."** Must complete the loop (not just press play and abandon). N is **configurable**, starting at 3 during the whitelist phase. Tune based on observed user behavior.

**Why not pure rep count?** The funnel doc is unusually emphatic: _"Gating on a quota builds a treadmill that rewards attendance over learning and demoralizes the stuck-intermediate avatar."_ "Played in full" is a softer version — it at least requires completion, not just clicks. We watch the gate closely once whitelist users are inside; if we see frustration at the wall, this is the first knob to tune.

**At each unlock**, the player chooses **harder · same · easier** + genre lean. Gives agency _and_ quietly feeds the future personalization engine. The "easier" branch is also the **release valve** — no one feels trapped.

## The weekly engine — one rep, four jobs

Once a week we create **one original bass groove** — our own drums, our own keys — and it does four jobs at once:

1. The YouTube video
2. The free funnel groove (new this week)
3. The retention hook (a reason to come back every week)
4. A brick in the paid catalog

### Copyright: principle, not notes

_Inspired by_ a famous line — for the title and search traffic — but we **teach the principle behind it, not the notes of it.** Write a genuinely original groove that demonstrates that principle.

> **The test:** would a musicologist say "same line" (you copied) or "same trick, different line" (you're clear)? Aim for the second.

**Two copyrights to respect:** the _composition_ (notes) and the _recording_ (the master). The recording is the one Content ID auto-claims within minutes. **The original studio recording never goes in the timeline, and never into the platform.**

This matters more on the platform than on YouTube. A claimed video is one demonetized video. But shipping copyrighted audio _inside the product_ converts a clean 95%-margin software business into a music-licensing business — a different, far worse company. _Every groove, backing, drum, and key part we ship is original and owned._

## The email sequence — a rep in the inbox

Email is our **direct pipe** — the hedge against feeds dying. **A Bassicology email can't be a thing they read. It has to send them to their bass.**

Every email is a 5-minute rep: _"Open this. Loop it. Mute the bass. Play just the ghost notes for 5 minutes. Reply and tell me what you noticed."_

**Personalized from email one** — using assessment scores captured at signup.

The welcome arc (~14 days):

- **Days 1–5** — five grooves, five reps. Pure giving.
- **Day 6** — the reframe: _"You've done 5 reps this week. Most people who follow a bass account did zero."_ Consumer → player.
- **Days 7–8** — uncap rep → **Membership ($24/mo)**
- **Days 9–10** — technique rep → **Groove Pack ($39)**
- **Days 11–12** — the path → **4-Week Accelerator ($97)**
- **Day 13+** — weekly rhythm. New video groove = new email rep. Re-pitch on **triggers** (conquered a set, hit the ceiling, new pack ships), never a calendar.

**Founding Membership offer:** introduced _after_ open, not at first contact — once whitelist users have seen the platform is real, not a mirage. See "Phase 4" below.

## The product tiers — each sells a different verb

| Tier                                                  | Price   | Verb                        | What it is                                                              |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ------- | --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Free account**                                      | free    | **play**                    | Playable instrument + collect grooves with capped levers                |
| **Membership**                                        | $24/mo  | **practice without limits** | Caps come off everything (tempo, keys, loop) + widgets + notation       |
| **Funk Vol. 1 Pack**                                  | $39     | **understand → ability**    | 12 funk grooves level 1, drillable deconstruction layers                |
| **Economy Picking Accelerator**                       | $97     | **stop deciding**           | 30-day drip-fed practice-along videos + 1:1 cohort guidance + community |
| **(launch-phase, post-open)** **Founding Membership** | $397    | **patron**                  | First 100 founders. Lifetime Membership. Fund the build.                |
| **(future) 1:1 Coaching**                             | premium | **a person**                | The one thing software can't be                                         |

### The coherence test

Each tier must pass: _can a happy customer of the tier below still have an unmet need this tier serves?_

- Free user (happy playing) → "I keep hitting the ceiling." → **Membership** ✓
- Member (practicing unlimited) → "I understand these but still can't _nail_ the technique." → **Funk Pack** ✓
- Member + pack → "I don't know what to do each day, I keep drifting." → **Accelerator** ✓

### Understanding is free; ability is paid

The trap: YouTube _deconstructs_ the groove, and the Pack _sells_ the deconstruction — doesn't the video give the product away? No:

- **Understanding** — knowing _how_ the groove works. Cheap to give; it markets you.
- **Ability** — _being able to play it_ clean, at tempo, in any key. Understanding never produces ability — that's our company's entire premise.

> The gap between _understanding it_ and _being able to play it_ is the product. The video shows the X-ray; the Pack is the gym that builds the muscle.

### Founding Membership — what it covers

> **Lifetime = the recurring membership, forever — the room and everything that lands in it. NOT the à la carte products.**

- **Included:** unlimited practice + widgets + notation + every future _widget and platform improvement_ as it ships.
- **Separate, even for founders:** Groove Packs ($39 each) and the Accelerator ($97) stay à la carte. Folding them into a lifetime deal would exempt our 100 best customers from the entire product line built to monetize them.
- **Excluded:** 1:1 coaching.

The rule: **lifetime covers the _room_, not the _furniture you keep buying_.**

## The launch plan — four phases

### Phase 1 — Whitelist (starts this week, ~6 weeks)

**Goal:** Open the YouTube channel and start building the list.

**What ships immediately:**

- [ ] **YouTube channel opens.** First original groove video uploaded.

- Pack content (12 funk grooves) produced in parallel — this is your side work, no platform timeline pressure.

### Phase 2 — Build the funnel-pure platform (parallel with Phase 1)

**Goal:** What the whitelist will see when we open. The big work.

#### 2a — The capped lever system (THE core build)

Without this, the free tier is either "fully open" (no funnel pitch) or "fully locked" (no AHA). This is the highest-priority build.

- [ ] **Tempo cap** — free users can step to one slower preset and back. Members get the full 40–200 BPM dial.
- [ ] **Transpose cap** — free users shift to one other key. Members get all 12.
- [ ] **Mute-once** — free users can mute the bass once and jam 8 bars. Members loop infinitely.
- [ ] **Deconstruction visible-but-grayed** — free users see the layers exist. Members isolate and drill them.
- [ ] **Each cap teaches its upsell** — clear messaging at the wall ("the full dial is in Membership"), never a fake locked button.

#### 2b — The "earn next groove" gate

- [ ] **"Played in full N times" detection** — must complete the loop, not just press play.
- [ ] **Configurable threshold N** — start at 3, dashboard knob to tune.
- [ ] **Unlock UI** — when threshold hit, show "harder · same · easier" + genre choice for next groove.
- [ ] **Release valve** — "easier" path always available; nobody trapped behind a gate.

#### 2c — Membership infrastructure (build & test FIRST)

The recurring heart and the simplest mechanically — validates the cap system end-to-end.

- [ ] **Stripe subscription product** — $24/mo Membership.
- [ ] **"is member?" check** that uncaps every lever.
- [ ] **Checkout flow** from any wall in the product.
- [ ] **Membership management** (cancel, billing portal).

#### 2d — Pack & Accelerator infrastructure

- [ ] **Pack purchasing** + per-pack content access (Funk Vol. 1).
- [ ] **Drip-feed infrastructure** for Accelerator (the ~16hr backend work from BETA Plan):
  - `tutorial_releases` table — (user_id, purchase_id, tutorial_id, scheduled_at)
  - `DripFeedService` — calculates per-user availability
  - Tutorial access guard in controller
  - Frontend: "today's tutorial" / "next unlocks in X days"
- [ ] **Bundle entitlements** — webhook grants Membership + Pack + Accelerator combinations correctly.
- [ ] **Community chat for Accelerator cohort** — Discord or Slack invite, _not_ custom-built. Don't build chat.

#### 2e — Polish carried over from BETA Plan

- [ ] **Single play-button loading spinner** (not 4 toasts).
- [ ] **Sample preload on page mount** (not first user gesture).
- [ ] **Retry-with-backoff on Supabase fetch failures** (use existing CircuitBreaker).
- [ ] **iOS Safari banner** — "Best experience on desktop. Mobile coming soon."
- [ ] **Hide unfinished features from nav** — Groove Finder, Journey, Patterns, Social (already partially done).
- [ ] **Landing page copy rewrite** — drop "personalized practice," lead with "tutorial library + play-along practice." (Public landing, not the whitelist page.)

## Risks and what we're watching

### Risk 1 — The "played in full N times" gate may frustrate

The funnel doc explicitly warns against quota gates. Our version is softer (requires completion, not clicks), but it's still not Bridge. **Watch closely once whitelist users are inside.** First knob to tune: N. Second knob: whether to bypass the gate for the "easier" path.

### Risk 2 — One-person 1:1 Accelerator doesn't scale

You're handling the first cohort hands-on, with a community chat to help them "graduate together." Sustainable for the first cohort or two. Beyond that, the community chat + async video feedback + occasional group sessions becomes the model. **Cap first-cohort enrollment to a manageable size.**

### Risk 3 — Free collection without Bridge could feel like hoarding

The funnel doc says collecting is the dream, not the danger — but the framing assumes capped levers + Bridge gates make every free groove a _demo_ of the wall. Without Bridge, the wall is softer. **Mitigation:** the cap system itself is the wall. Each free groove is still a sixty-times-confronted demo of the tempo cap, transpose cap, mute-once.

### Risk 4 — Carry-over technical debt from the audit

These are deprioritized but tracked, from the prior BETA Plan:

- 6 different "is samples ready?" sources of truth — fragile but not broken.
- Per-exercise bass buffer reloading — verify caching is right.
- ~6.7k legacy lint errors — already deferred, no per-commit friction.
- 35 undocumented env vars — pure polish.
- Sentry verification on next staging deploy.
- Segment-level `error.tsx` instead of just root.
